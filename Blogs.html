Want to secure great Internships? IEEE Student’s Branch NIT Durgapur presents

“The Internship Bootcamp”

Today all engineers irrespective of their branch or year of study have to face one big challenge: how do you increase your chances of securing a good internship be it here or overseas? The answer is deceptively simple actually. You stay updated regarding opportunities, apply early and apply to as many places as possible. We all dream of securing an internship at our dream company or institute. We all know the importance of getting a good internship since it’s the first time you experience the limelight of the world (corporate or research ) to which you wish to commit the rest of your life. Your internship will prove to be a pivotal point in your career. It gives you real-world experience, enhances your network, augments your resume, makes you a master at time management and most importantly you discover new things about yourself. The internship you pursue is going to be one of the most important building blocks of your career foundation. So shouldn’t it match up to your expectations? So We, IEEE Students Branch NIT Durgapur, organized “The Internship BootCamp”, which was conducted on the 8th and 9th of August, 2018 at DM Sen Conference Hall. The workshop was aimed at providing quality exposure to students regarding fellowship opportunities, undergrad projects, and internships in and around the country as well as abroad. On the first day, the workshop started off with a brief introduction about the IEEE society and its impactful mission in providing students a platform to establish their careers. It was followed by a brief presentation given by Abhishek Singh, third year, Department of CSE on the various foreign internships open to Indian students, their eligibility criteria, and the respective procedures. Having spiked the audience’s attention with the right amount of motivation, we launched our ‘Experience Talks’ session where we brought in some of our Final Year students who had bagged foreign internships last year and were associated with multiple research projects.
